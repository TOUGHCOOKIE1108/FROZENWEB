FROZEN

  • The relationship between let it go and frozen

    • This song is not only Elsa's monologue but also advances the story. In the song, she builds her ice castle, symbolizing her decision to let go of the past and embrace independence. However, her release of self inadvertently affects others, such as the Kingdom of Arendelle.

  • Introduction of the music

    • The song begins in F# minor, conveying Elsa's unease, then shifts to A major in the chorus, symbolizing an emotional release and journey from darkness to light, aligning with her personal growth. The conclusion ends on a high note, representing confidence and self-belonging. The melody spans about two octaves, starting with a piano solo that evokes loneliness. Strings, percussion, and chorus gradually join, building intensity to symbolize emotional accumulation and release. The chorus incorporates powerful percussion and brass, injecting energy and grandeur. The rhythm evolves from slow to faster and becomes steady 4/4 in the chorus, enhancing stability and strength.

  • The relationship between into the unknown and frozen

    • The song reflects Elsa's curiosity and fear of the inner calling and her yearning for an unknown adventure. It drives the plot by revealing Elsa's journey to uncover the truth and the source of her magic while embodying themes of courage, growth, and self-discovery.

  • Introduction of the music

    • The first section, referred to as the siren, mirrors Greek mythology's sirens, known for luring sailors with their voices. The melody draws on the famous "Dies Irae" motif, conveying mystery and allure.

    Though based in D major, the song uses unstable chords and modes like dissonance and secondary dominants to create suspense and uncertainty. It begins so ly with piano, gradually adding strings and percussion, with brass and strong drum beats in the chorus creating emotional tension. Irregular rhythm and free tempo in the main melody mimic the elusive inner call, while the chorus returns to a steady 4/4 rhythm, emphasizing strength and resolution.
